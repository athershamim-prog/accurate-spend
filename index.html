The Challenge

Is Your Aggregate Spend Reporting Process Holding You Back?

Under the Physician Payments Sunshine Act, pharmaceutical manufacturers must report all transfers of value to healthcare professionals to CMS annually. Without the right solution, this becomes a significant compliance burden.

Fragmented, inconsistent data sources

Spend data scattered across CRMs, ERPs, expense tools, and spreadsheets with no unified format.

Manual validation is slow and error-prone

Teams spend weeks cleaning and reconciling data — still at risk of CMS submission errors.

Enterprise software is prohibitively expensive

Legacy platforms like MediSpend charge licensing fees most mid-market pharma companies can't justify.

Non-compliance carries serious CMS penalties

Inaccurate or late Open Payments filings can result in fines up to $1.18M per year.

The Sunshine Act at a Glance

1

Who Must Report

Pharmaceutical manufacturers, medical device companies & GPOs with products covered by Medicare, Medicaid, or CHIP.

2

What Must Be Reported

All transfers of value to covered HCPs and teaching hospitals — meals, speaker fees, consulting, travel, research, education, and more.

3

CMS Annual Deadline

Reports must be submitted to CMS via Open Payments each year — typically by March 31st for the prior calendar year.

4

Penalty Risk

Civil monetary penalties range from $1,000–$10,000 per payment not reported, up to $1.18M annually.
